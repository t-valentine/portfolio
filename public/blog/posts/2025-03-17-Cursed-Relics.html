Cursed Relics Post-Mortem

What is Cursed Relics?

Cursed Relics is a drawing challenge from ARTIST. You can find the 2024 prompt list here: LINK.

I wanted to draw a little introductory 4 page comic, then do illustrations for the remaining prompts and maybe draw a comic as the 'end'. It was very much originally planned as a series of illustrations. The 'final' work is 4 pages of illustrations and 29 pages of comics.

Somewhere along the way (mid October), I began to want to draw more comics and tell a real, coherent story rather than random vignettes. In addition to this, I thought it would be run to update the comic bi-weekly as a short form webcomic. I've always loved webcomics and have been interested in them as a medium; this seemed like a fun way to try my hand at it without overcommitting.

How Was It?

Fun!!!

I hosted the comic on this site using Rarebit. I used an edited GitHub action to autodeploy updates twice a week (which allowed me to "queue" one page at a time rather than needing to update manually every day). I kind of wish I'd worked a little more and found a way to queue multiple pages though. (Admittedly, this is mostly a me problem. I only have a desktop computer and had to leave my house for a week, so I couldn't code or queue a new page since I didn't have a computer.)

What Did I Learn?

  • Planning is important
    (I knew I was underplanning the comic, but there's a lot more to planning besides 'plot' and 'thumbnails'.)
  • B&W comics are hard to keep from looking muddled (color is easier for my brain I guess)
  • I really need a 2 month buffer (technically I need a 1 month buffer and then a 1 month buffer for my buffer)
  • If I want to draw stuff besides the comic, my (current) limit is 1 page a week

Anything else?

I'm a bit torn between hosting my comic somewhere like Comic Fury, or continuing to use my own free website with Rarebit. I'll probably play around with Comic Fury a bit. I do feel like Rarebit is super useful and free and totally up to me to customize, BUT. It's really hard for me to have to be at my desk to update. Also, I really like the idea of being able to bulk upload and schedule pages.
